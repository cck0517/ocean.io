About Our Oceans

Deep Waters

The ocean covers 71% of Earth's surface and contains 97% of Earth's water.

Marine Life

The ocean is home to millions of known species, with many more yet to be discovered.

Ecosystem

Oceans produce over 50% of the world's oxygen and absorb carbon dioxide.

Ocean Ecosystems

Coral Reefs

Often called the "rainforests of the sea," coral reefs support approximately 25% of all marine species while covering less than 1% of the ocean floor. These diverse ecosystems are vital for marine life and protect coastlines from storms.

Deep Ocean Zones

The deep ocean, starting at 200 meters below surface, houses some of Earth's most fascinating creatures. Despite extreme pressure and darkness, these zones contain unique life forms adapted to these harsh conditions.

Kelp Forests

These underwater forests can grow up to 100 feet tall and are among the most productive ecosystems on Earth. They provide food and shelter for thousands of fish, invertebrates, and marine mammal species.

Arctic Waters

The Arctic Ocean, Earth's smallest and shallowest ocean, plays a crucial role in regulating global climate. Its unique ecosystem supports specialized species like polar bears, seals, and various whale species.

Mangrove Forests

These coastal trees create critical nursery habitats for many marine species, protect coastlines from erosion, and help filter water. They also serve as important carbon sinks, storing more carbon than terrestrial forests.

Open Ocean

The pelagic zone, or open ocean, is the largest habitat on Earth. This vast expanse supports diverse life forms from tiny plankton to the largest animal ever known - the blue whale.
